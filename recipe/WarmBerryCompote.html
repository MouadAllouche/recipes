Warm Berry Compote

Warm Berry Compote is a delightful and versatile dessert sauce that celebrates the natural sweetness and tartness of mixed berries

This recipe combines fresh or frozen berries—such as strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries—with a touch of sugar and a hint of lemon zest, gently simmered to create a luscious, syrupy compote. The result is a vibrant, ruby-red sauce that can be enjoyed in a multitude of ways.

Perfect for topping pancakes, waffles, or French toast at breakfast, Warm Berry Compote also shines as an elegant addition to desserts like cheesecake, ice cream, or panna cotta. Its warm, comforting flavor makes it a wonderful choice for cozy gatherings and special occasions alike. The simplicity of this recipe, combined with the natural goodness of the berries, makes Warm Berry Compote a go-to for adding a burst of fruity flavor to any dish.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/2 cup fresh lime juice (about 4 limes)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth

Steps

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a small bowl, combine the lime juice, chopped cilantro, olive oil, minced garlic, ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to create the marinade.
  2. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken breasts in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring each piece is well coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for a stronger flavor.
  3. Set Up the Slow Cooker: Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the slow cooker to prevent sticking and add extra moisture during cooking.
  4. Add the Chicken: Remove the chicken breasts from the marinade and place them in the slow cooker. Discard any remaining marinade.
  5. Cook: Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  6. Serve: Once cooked, remove the chicken from the slow cooker. You can serve the chicken whole, or shred it with two forks for a different presentation. Garnish with additional fresh cilantro and lime wedges if desired.
  7. Enjoy: Serve the Slow Cooker Lime Cilantro Chicken over rice, in tacos, or alongside your favorite sides.
